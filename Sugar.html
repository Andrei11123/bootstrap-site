Sugar Glider

The Sugar Glider is a small nocturnal marsupial found in forests of Australia and New Guinea. Known for its ability to glide, it is a social creature often kept as a pet.

Habitat

Sugar Gliders live in forests and woodlands, gliding between trees to travel.

Diet

Their diet includes nectar, fruits, and insects.

Reproduction

Sugar Gliders are social and often live in groups. They give birth to one or two young per year.

About the Sugar Glider

This charming marsupial is admired for its gliding skills and social nature. While they make great pets, they need proper care due to their specific dietary and social needs.
